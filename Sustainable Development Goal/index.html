Sustainable Development Goals – India


What are SDGs?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a universal call to action by the United Nations to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity for all by 2030. India, as a committed member of the UN, has been actively working towards achieving these 17 goals through various national programmes. This portal highlights India’s progress, plans, and partnerships to build a more inclusive, sustainable, and resilient future for every Indian.

Why are SDGs important for India?

India plays a key role in achieving the global SDG targets. With its large and diverse population, sustainable development is essential to improving lives and protecting natural resources. Through national missions like Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, Digital India, and Ujjwala Yojana, India is working towards clean energy, quality education, gender equality, and more. Achieving these goals will lead to a healthier, more inclusive, and stronger India.

Role of Computer Science & AI in SDGs

Artificial Intelligence and Computer Science are revolutionizing the way we approach development goals. From analyzing climate patterns and delivering e-learning to tracking disease outbreaks and managing data-driven decisions, they enable faster, smarter, and more inclusive solutions.

In India, AI-powered solutions support agriculture, education, health, and infrastructure. This digital transformation ensures that the path to 2030 is guided by innovation and intelligent systems.
