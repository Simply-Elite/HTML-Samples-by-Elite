Kanda Poha Recipe

What is Kanda Poha?
The Name itself describes the main components:
  • Kanda means Onion (in Marathi).
  • Poha means Flattened Rice (or rice flakes).
What is the Flavor Profile of the Dish?
This dish has a vibrant and balanced taste that is Savory, Mildly Spicy (from green chilies), Citrusy (from a squeeze of lemon juice), and sometimes slightly Sweet (from the sauteed onions or tomato ketchup).
How does the Dish Look?
The dish typically appears beautiful when served with perfection: sunny yellow due to turmeric and other rich colors from the ingredients added. It is garnished with fresh, finely chopped coriander leaves.

Ingredients Required:

  • 2 cups Thick Poha (Flattened Rice)
  • 1 large Onion, finely chopped (Kanda)
  • 1/4 cup Roasted Peanuts
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds & Curry Leaves
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder, Salt, Sugar (optional)
  • Fresh Lemon Juice & Coriander for Garnish

Preparation Steps:

  1. Rinse the Poha in water for a few seconds and drain. Set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add curry leaves.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add turmeric, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  5. Add the rinsed Poha and mix gently. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Garnish with lemon juice and coriander before serving.
